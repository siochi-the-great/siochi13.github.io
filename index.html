The Flow of My Music and My Life

Childhood Years

     Hi! I'm Franz Josef Siochi I was born in Malabon City, a coastal city in Metro Manila.1 My childhood days were one of the best moments of my life.2 These were the times when my friends would invite me to play outside—games like tagu-taguan and patintero.3 I also played role-playing games like bahay-bahayan with my cousins at home.4 We would listen to and sing along to jejemon songs together.5 After that, we would just watch TV and enjoy our time.6 This was also the time I was introduced to computers.7 I remember seeing my first computer, and my dad was using it.8 It was fascinating to watch.9 Around this time, I was also introduced to video games.10 I would watch my dad play, observing everything before trying it myself.11 Pero nakalaro din ako ng laro sa computer.12 My first game was Command & Conquer: Generals, which is still my favorite game.13 Nagustuhan ko kasi yung gameplay at yung uniqueness ng game, and that's why nagka-interest din ako sa history—especially war history.14 Pero noong time na yun, nasira yung computer namin, at nakita ko yung components, which I found very, very interesting—even until today.15 Pero for the meantime, sa comshop kami nagko-computer.16 Mura pa noon ang price rate ng comshop, 10 pesos per hour.17 Tapos doon ko rin nalaro yung second favorite game ko, which is GTA: San Andreas.18 The open-world experience of the game was so immersive and enjoyable. 19 I spent hours exploring, completing missions, and trying different things in the game.20

Teenage Years

     In my teenage years, when I was in Grade 6, I had this one friend—the only friend I had because I wasn’t really a social person.1 Lagi siyang nambuburaot sakin ng 5 pesos, but in exchange, he protected me since I was often bullied during that time.2 Grade 6 was definitely not my favorite time in life.3 Teachers still used slippers and long flat wood to punish students who were sleeping or interrupting their lectures.4 I remember seeing my classmates get punished, which I found sad.5 However, there was one moment in Grade 6 that I did like—graduation.6 It marked the end of that chapter and the start of high school life.7 In high school, I joined a marching music band.8 This was when I discovered my talent for playing the saxophone.9 My dad is a musician, so I basically inherited my talent from him.10 He wanted me to become a musician because some universities offer scholarships to talented individuals.11 Because of this, I enrolled at UE, following in my dad's footsteps, before later transferring to UST.12 During those years, I developed my musical skills while also gaining educational knowledge.13 Over time, I became more focused on learning—some might even say I became a nerd, which was fine with me.14 High school was a mix of challenges and great experiences.15 One of the highlights was performing at school events, which became some of my favorite moments from my teenage years.16 When I reached Grade 12, I auditioned for both Adamson and UE.17 At that time, I felt nervous, fearing I might not get accepted.18 But in the end, I passed both auditions.19 However, I chose UE because it was just one ride away, I knew many people there, and I was familiar with the head of the UE band.20

Collage Years

     In my college years, bago pa man ang first day ng pasok, may GC na yung very first block section ko.1 Nagplano sila ng meet-up, and for me, this was both exciting and nerve-wracking at the same time.2 Hindi ko alam paano makipagkaibigan kasi hindi naman ako marunong makipag-socialize.3 Pero okay lang, kasi nung nakita ko sila, dun ko naramdaman na parang kaya ko naman makipag-socialize.4 Mas gusto ko pa rin yung ka-humor ko, at buti na lang meron naman.5 Nung first day of school, nag-usap-usap na kami, and after that, lumabas kami para kumain nang sabay-sabay.6 Ito yung moment sa buhay ko na nagkaroon ako ng madaming friends.7 Hindi ko in-expect na may ka-humor pala ako.8 Simula noon, mas naging madali sakin ang makipag-usap sa iba.9 Araw-araw kaming nagtatawanan at nagkukwentuhan.10 Sa experience ko, college is a challenging time.11 Minsan malulungkot ka kasi hindi ka pumasa sa quiz.12 Maraming academic activities na mahirap ipasa.13 Pero masaya rin kasi yung mga kaibigan mo ang nagpapasaya sayo.14 Walang ending na tawanan at kwentuhan.15 Tapos magkakaroon ka pa ng bagong crush, na minsan nagiging motivation mo sa pagpasok.16 Para sa akin, ito ang best time ng buhay ko.17 Kahit mahirap at puno ng challenges, worth it pa rin.18 Dito ko nakita na hindi ka kailanman magiging mag-isa.19 Ang college ay hindi lang tungkol sa pag-aaral kundi sa pagbuo ng tunay na pagkakaibigan.20 Natutunan ko rin kung paano maging independent at mas confident sa sarili.21
